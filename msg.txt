where choices can be made that lead to different (but all valid) results.
I’m writing to you because, in my research on how Ghidra’s decompiler works, your name has come up several times;

the final output in a fairly substantial way without overcomplicating things.

At the moment I’ve modified the decompiler so that it can return 2 versions, the original one and another one with the changes.
I’ve created a new “group” (like decompile, normalize, jumptable...) and, thanks to a Ghidra extension, I obtain both decompiled versions of a function.
I’ve also currently implemented, as a choice of my new “SimplificationStyle” (this is what the group is called in Java), one of your pull requests, namely
the “Improve for-loop detection”, and one of my own changes in ruleBlockWhileDo that transforms if(cond){do{..}while(cond)} into while(cond){}
